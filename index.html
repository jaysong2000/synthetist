Parental Figures are Crucial to a Person
Wes Moore
  • Put into a rich private school, and is one of the few black kids who attend the school. Therefore, Wes puts in a great effort to appear rich for the school and tough for his friends in the Bronx.

    Because of this, Wes is forced to live a life between the two lives, resulting in his grades dipping and increasing apathy towards his studies.
  • It is revealed that Wes Moore has increasinly suffered from low grades, but is simply not trying, due to him just not caring anymore. His mother threatens him with military school due to this.
  • When Wes Moore is arrested with his friend Shea (who deals drugs) for vandalism (spraypainting on the wall), he, unlike the nonchalant Shea, fears for what will happen to him, showing remorse of his actions (although he returns to his antics later, he at least is concerned about the consequences).
The Other Wes Moore
  • Wes grows envious of his brother Tony who is able to afford luxurious and new clothing due to his job as a drug dealer, despite Tony's attempts at making Wes go a different path.
  • Wes finds Marijuana in his mom's closet, and, despite knowing that it is illegal, brings it to his friends anyways. Not only this, Wes goes directly against his brother's requests and becomes a drug dealer.Despite returning with the scent of drugs and alcohol, Wes' mother naively hopes this is a one time thing.
  • When his mom finds out about Wes' income from dealing drugs, she angrily throws out his shoes and drugs. This angers Wes, as that was a lot of money lost. Instead of reconsidering his actions, he runs to his girlfriend's house and sets up his shop there.
From this, it is evident that although the two Wes Moores both got into trouble relating to drugs or fighting and whatnot when they were children, their reactions were much different from each other. The author, although not completely dropping their troublesome actions (as implied in the end of Chapter 4 where he goes back to "tagging"), at least felt some remorse for his actions, while that was not seen in the other Wes Moore. The author uses these subtle behavior differences to show that despite the similarities in the conditions the two Wes Moores faced, they will go on vastly different paths in the future. Another key difference between the two Wes Moores is the reaction from their mother. The author's mother is strict, and threatens Wes with military school after minor infractions such as low grades, while the other Wes Moore's mother do not act until Wes' actions are in front of her eyes - and she fails to listen to Tony, or discipline Wes the first time she realizes he smells like alcohol and marijuana. These differences prove to have a great impact on the personality of the two Wes Moores, as the author has a form of control over himself, while the other Wes Moore does not - contributing to their polar opposite futures.
